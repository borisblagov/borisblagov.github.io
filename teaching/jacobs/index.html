Financial Crises in the 21-st century

Info

When: Friday, 05.04.2019 9:45 to 18:30 Friday, 26.04.2019 11:15 to 18:30 Saturday, 26.04.2019 9:45 to 18:30

​ Where: West Hall

​ Material: In campus net.

​Mainstream economics is about isolating key questions that may be analysed in a theoretical framework. In practice the economy is an extremely complex global phenomenon, where everything happens as an outcome of the interaction of millions of people. The goal of this course is to provide a bridge between the theory and practice in economics from the perspective of the global financial crisis. By employing knowledge from various aspects of economics, as they are presented in the first and second years of study you will learn: (i) How even the most basic macroeconomic model can describe the challenges faced by the policy-makers throughout the global financial crisis. (ii) How the crisis unfolded and what made it different than previous economic downturns. (iii) How and why the different countries reacted differently to the crisis, what “unconventional monetary policy” is and how that might be the future of policy-makers.
